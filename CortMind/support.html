How to Use Cort Mind

🚀 1. Getting Started

Step 1 — Open Cort Mind and tap New Session in the sidebar to create your first thinking session.
Step 2 — Give your session a name that describes the problem or decision you're thinking about.
Step 3 — Tap any sector on the CoRT Wheel to open that thinking tool.
Step 4 — Fill in the structured fields to organize your thinking systematically.
Step 5 — Use Apple Pencil to add handwritten notes and diagrams in the canvas area.
Step 6 — Tap Save to add your thinking record to the session.

🧠 2. CoRT Thinking Tools

Cort Mind provides six structured thinking tools based on the CoRT methodology:

📊 PMI (Plus, Minus, Interesting)

Systematically analyze any idea by examining its positive aspects, negative aspects, and interesting implications.

  • Plus — What are the advantages and benefits?
  • Minus — What are the disadvantages and problems?
  • Interesting — What are the interesting implications and side effects?

🔍 CAF (Consider All Factors)

Ensure you consider every relevant factor before making a decision.

  • Core Factors — Key factors that directly affect the decision
  • Easily Ignored — Important factors often overlooked
  • Potential Factors — Hidden factors that may have impact

⏰ C&S (Consequences & Sequel)

Think through outcomes across different time horizons.

  • Immediate (now) — Effects that happen right away
  • Short-term (1-3 mo) — Changes in the next few months
  • Medium-term (3-12 mo) — Developments over the next year
  • Long-term (1 yr+) — Far-reaching effects beyond a year

🎯 AGO (Aims, Goals, Objectives)

Clarify all goals and constraints upfront to prevent vague direction.

  • Main Goal — The primary objective you want to achieve
  • Secondary Goal — Supporting goals that assist the main goal
  • Hidden Goal — Goals you may not have stated explicitly
  • Constraints — Time, cost, resource or other limitations

🔄 APC (Alternatives, Possibilities, Choices)

Break fixed thinking and expand the solution space.

  • Mainstream — Traditional, safe, lower-risk options
  • Innovative — Novel, creative alternatives
  • High-Risk — Bold choices with high risk and reward
  • Optimal — Best choice after weighing all options

📋 Action Planning

Convert thinking into concrete steps for effective execution.

Use this tool to break down your decisions into actionable tasks with timelines and responsibilities.

🤖 3. AI Analysis (Optional)

Cort Mind can provide AI-powered analysis of your thinking using Anthropic's Claude AI:

Step 1 — Get an API key from Anthropic's console (requires free account)
Step 2 — In Cort Mind, go to Settings → Anthropic API Key
Step 3 — Enter your API key and tap Save API Key
Step 4 — Back in any thinking tool, tap AI Analysis to get AI feedback
💡 Note: AI analysis is completely optional. The app works fully offline without it. You only pay for what you use through your Anthropic account.

📁 4. Managing Sessions

Sessions help you organize related thinking over time:

  • Create a session — Tap New Session in the sidebar
  • Switch sessions — Tap any session name in the sidebar
  • Delete a session — Swipe left on the session name and tap Delete
  • Export sessions — Tap the share icon in the session header to export as JSON or CSV

✏️ 5. Apple Pencil & Handwriting

Cort Mind supports Apple Pencil for handwritten notes and diagrams:

  • Write naturally — Use your Apple Pencil to write or draw in the canvas area
  • Clear canvas — Tap Clear Canvas to erase all handwritten content
  • Save to Photos — Tap Save to Photos to export your notes as an image
💡 Works best on iPad with Apple Pencil, but you can also use your finger on iPhone or non-Pencil iPad.

📤 6. Export & Sharing

Share your thinking sessions in multiple formats:

  • JSON — Structured data format for backup or importing elsewhere
  • CSV — Spreadsheet format for analysis in Excel or Google Sheets
  • Image — Save handwritten notes to your photo library

⚙️ 7. Settings

  • Anthropic API Key — Enter your API key for AI analysis
  • Recommended Order — Customize the CoRT wheel numbering to match your preferred sequence
  • Reset Tutorial — Replay the onboarding tutorial

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the app ask for photo library permission?

Cort Mind only accesses your photo library when you explicitly choose to save handwritten notes as images. We never read or upload any existing photos from your library. You can revoke this permission anytime in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos → Cort Mind.

Where is my data stored?

All your thinking sessions, notes, and preferences are stored locally on your device. Nothing is automatically sent to any server. The only exception is when you choose to use AI analysis - in that case, your thinking content is sent to Anthropic's servers using your personal API key.

Do I need an internet connection to use Cort Mind?

No! Cort Mind works completely offline. The only feature that requires internet is the optional AI analysis, and even that can use cached responses when offline.

How does the AI analysis work?

When you enable AI analysis, your thinking content is sent to Anthropic's Claude AI using your personal API key. The AI provides structured feedback on your thinking. You only pay for what you use through your Anthropic account, and the app works fine without this feature.

Is my API key secure?

Yes. Your Anthropic API key is stored securely in the iOS Keychain on your device. It's only transmitted directly to Anthropic's servers when you use AI analysis - we never see, store, or transmit your key to any other service.

Can I recover deleted sessions?

No. Deleted sessions are permanently removed from your device. We recommend exporting important sessions as JSON or CSV before deleting them.

The app crashed / something isn't working. What should I do?

Try force-quitting and relaunching the app. If the problem persists, please email us at daibou007@gmail.com with a description of the issue and your device model & iOS version.

What are the best practices for using CoRT tools?

For best results: use PMI for quick evaluations, CAF for important decisions, C&S for planning, AGO for goal setting, APC for creative problem solving, and Action Planning for implementation. You can use them in sequence for comprehensive thinking or individually as needed.
