How to Use TintSnap

📷 1. Sampling a Color with the Camera

Step 1 — Open the app and tap the Camera tab at the bottom.
Step 2 — Point your camera at any surface or object whose color you want to capture.
Step 3 — A crosshair appears in the center of the screen. Move your phone so the crosshair is over the exact color you want.
Step 4 — Tap the large circular Capture button at the bottom of the screen. TintSnap instantly reads the color under the crosshair.
Step 5 — The color swatch panel slides up showing the sampled color and its values (HEX, RGB, HSL, CMYK). Tap any value to copy it to your clipboard.
💡 Tip: For best accuracy, ensure good lighting and hold the phone steady before tapping Capture.

🎨 2. Switching Color Formats

After sampling a color, the info panel shows multiple formats. Tap the format name (HEX / RGB / HSL / CMYK) to cycle through them, or tap any individual value to copy it directly.

  • HEX — e.g. #6C5CE7 — for web and CSS
  • RGB — e.g. rgb(108, 92, 231) — for design tools
  • HSL — e.g. hsl(249°, 76%, 63%) — great for color adjustments
  • CMYK — e.g. cmyk(53%, 60%, 0%, 9%) — for print design

You can set your preferred default format in Settings → Default Color Format.

🖌 3. Creating and Managing Palettes

The Palettes tab contains two sections:

  • Default — built-in curated palettes you can browse for inspiration.
  • My Palettes — your own saved palettes.

To create a new palette:

  1. Go to Palettes → My Palettes.
  2. Tap the ＋ button in the top-right corner.
  3. Give your palette a name.
  4. Add colors by sampling with the camera or entering a HEX value manually.
  5. Tap Save.

To add a color to an existing palette from the camera screen, sample a color and tap Add to Palette in the info panel, then choose the target palette.

To edit or delete a palette, long-press a palette card in My Palettes and choose Edit or Delete from the context menu.

To share a palette, open the palette detail view and tap the share icon. You can export as an image swatch, JSON, or CSS variables.

🕐 4. Color History

Every color you sample is automatically saved to the History tab (up to the last 100 colors). Tap any entry to view its full details and copy its values. History is stored locally on your device and is never uploaded anywhere.

💡 You can disable automatic history saving in Settings → Auto-save to History.

🌈 5. Color Harmony Generator

TintSnap can generate harmonious color schemes from any sampled color:

  1. Sample a color with the camera.
  2. In the color info panel, tap Generate Harmony.
  3. Choose a harmony type: Complementary, Triadic, Analogous, or Split-Complementary.
  4. The generated palette appears on screen. Tap Save to add it to My Palettes.

🏠 6. Paint Code Matching

TintSnap can match a sampled color to real-world paint codes (Pantone, RAL, Benjamin Moore):

  1. Sample a color with the camera.
  2. In the color info panel, tap Find Paint Match.
  3. TintSnap displays the closest matching paint codes with brand, code number, and color name.
  4. Tap a match to copy the paint code to your clipboard.

⚙️ 7. Settings

  • Default Color Format — sets which format (HEX, RGB, HSL, CMYK) is shown first after sampling.
  • Auto-save to History — toggle automatic saving of sampled colors.
  • Haptic Feedback — toggle vibration feedback when capturing a color.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the app ask for camera permission?

TintSnap uses the camera exclusively to sample colors from the real world. No photos are taken, saved, or uploaded. You can revoke camera access anytime in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera → TintSnap.

Where is my data stored?

All palettes, color history, and preferences are stored locally on your device. Nothing is sent to any server. Deleting the app will remove all stored data.

The color doesn't look accurate — what can I do?

Color accuracy depends on lighting conditions and camera calibration. For best results: use neutral white lighting, avoid glare and shadows, hold the phone close to the surface, and keep the crosshair perfectly still before tapping Capture.

Can I recover deleted palettes?

No. Deleted palettes are permanently removed from your device. We recommend exporting important palettes before deleting them.

How do I export a palette?

Open the palette detail view → tap the share icon (top-right) → choose your export format: Image Swatch (PNG), JSON, or CSS Variables.

The app crashed / something isn't working. What should I do?

Try force-quitting and relaunching the app. If the problem persists, please email us at daibou007@gmail.com with a description of the issue and your device model & iOS version.
